# Biblical Jurisprudence

A research project to systematically identify and analyze references to the Bible — and related religious language — in U.S. state and federal judicial rulings.

## Project Overview

Courts in the United States operate under a secular constitutional framework, yet religious language and scripture occasionally appear in judicial opinions. This project aims to surface, catalog, and analyze those references to better understand how — and how often — biblical and religious concepts enter the formal record of American law.

## Research Goals

- **Identify** every instance in which a state or federal judge references the Bible or related religious texts in a written ruling
- **Catalog** the context of each reference (case type, court level, jurisdiction, year, judge)
- **Expand** beyond the word "Bible" to capture related terms, phrases, and allusions (to be defined as the project develops)
- **Analyze** patterns across jurisdictions, time periods, and case types

## Scope

- U.S. federal courts (district, circuit, Supreme Court)
- U.S. state courts (trial and appellate levels)
- Written opinions and rulings only

## Search Terms (Initial — Subject to Expansion)

> The full list of search terms will be developed iteratively. Initial candidates include "Bible", "Biblical", "Scripture", and related terms. Expansion of this vocabulary is a key early task.
